Smart Home Solution for Bedridden!

What is MobiGo?

An effective Smart Home solution: MobiGO in aid of bedridden people in their domestic life. Smart home solutions have become a convenient system for everyone in their day-to-day activities. As the existing solutions do not cover every aspect of smart home solutions for bedridden people, a new gesture-driven solution for bedridden people is to be implemented. Apart from main smart home operations, some other major services are to be provided and they are, activity monitoring and abnormal behavior identification of the patient and IoT socket to be plugged to control the domestic devices.

Services We Provide

Hand Gesture Recognition

We present an improved Gesture Tracking and Recognition algorithm to recognize the sensitive gestures done by the bedridden people and communicate with the smart appliance connected with relevant signals. Compatible with normal users also.

Activity Monitoring

We present an improved activity monitoring algorithm to monitor and predict daily activities like Sleeping or not and exercised or not. A daily summary of the patient’s activities will be alerted to the guardian user.

Abnormal Behavior Detection

We present an improved abnormal behavior identification algorithm to monitor and predict abnormal activities like seizure or falling. Abnormal behaviors of the patient will be alerted to the guardian user if the patient has encountered with abnormal behavior.

Smart Socket

After capturing the specific gesture from the patient, it is compulsory to send command to the smart devices to do the specific task. We present the smart socket which is the bridge between s mart home and target user.

Literature Review

Smart Home System or Intelligent Home means in-house electrical appliance automation and control. Recent technological advances in electronic devices have resulted in a huge number of user-friendly and flexible systems being produced that have promoted smart living technology.

In the research done by Mehedi Hasan, Parag Biswas, MD Islam Bilash and Ashik Zafar of North South University Dhaka, Bangladesh on a Comparative analysis on smart home systems [19], they have compared the smart home developing technologies like DTMF, GSM (smartphone based), speech recognition-based, Zigbee-based, Bluetooth-based, Internet and Wi-Fi-based, and home automation solutions based on Gesture was studied [19]. The concept of remote control of devices in home environment originated with the use of smartphone with DTMF sound. The system used specific signal from the smartphone to carry out specific functions. The process requires the transmission of signal by a mobile phone and a DTMF transmitter. 5 DTMF based home automation presented in [20] is vulnerable to noise. Hence, usage of a mobile device is compulsory in order to give inputs to the smart home system, the compatibility of the system with our target user is not satisfactory.

R. Teymourzadeh, Salah Addin Ahmed, Kok Wai Chan and Mok Vee Hoong from Faculty of Engineering, Technology & Built Environment, UCSI University, Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia have developed a home automation system that uses mobile phone and GSM technology to control the smart home [23]. Due to the widespread coverage, GSM is used for the communication between home appliances and the user. Another advantage they took to count is the high security [23]. The main controlling device for this smart home is the smartphone. As mentioned earlier, usage of a mobile device is compulsory in order to give inputs to the smart home system, the compatibility of the system with our target user is not compatible. The GSM based proposed system have gained a 98% test accuracy with maximum four loads of tests.

Progress Mtshali and Freedom Khubisa, Department of Information Technology, ICT and Society Research Group, Durban University of Technology, Durban, South Africa have developed “A Smart Home Appliance Control System for Physically Disabled People” [2]. They have proposed a voice driven smart home solution to control home appliances. The main drawback of this solution is that disabled people like dumb people face difficulties due to inability to speak in order to give command to the system [3]. The user needs to use a microphone in order give commands to the smart home. For the voice capturing and storing, v3 module and Arduino is used. It converts the voice commands given by the user to a data structure and processes it. The data collected from the sensors was transmitted to the cloud for app analysis and usage. The drawback of this smart home is that users must learn commands to control the system, and the system's complexity makes it harder especially for disabled people to use.

Md. Ahsan Iqbal and others from Computer Science and Engineering Discipline, Khulna University, Khulna, Bangladesh have developed a similar smart home solution which is driven by gesture capturing sensors (kinect) [4]. It is essential to wear the data glove in order to give commands using gestures to the system. The kinect sensors are used to pick up the gesture send them to the central control unit to be converted to commands for the appliances. The main drawback of this system is that the object detection distance of the sensors is limited. And also, the sensitivity of the sensors for sunlight is high so gesture detection may not work properly.

But for the report of World Health Organization, about 15% of the world's population lives with some form of disability, of whom 2-4% experience significant difficulties in functioning [1]. Elderly people and People with disabilities like immobility, paralysis, deaf and dumb face many difficulties while trying to adapt to this kind of normal smart homes which have not been designed in a way that is user friendly for differently abled people.

Objectives
Main Objective

The main objective of this research is to implement an effective solution: MobiGO in aid of immobile people in their domestic life. Smart home solutions have become a convenient system for everyone in their day-to-day activities. As the existing solutions do not cover every aspect of smart home solutions for immobile people, a new gesturedriven solution for immobile people is to be implemented. Apart from main smart home operations, some other major services are to be provided and they are, activity monitoring and abnormal behavior identification of the patient and IoT socket to be plugged to control the domestic devices.

Sub Objective

Gesture Recognition Through Camera

As the ordinary gesture recognition algorithms are designed for ordinary people with no disabilities and inability to move [6]-[8][16], there are not many smart homes developed with the help of camera-based gesture recognition. And, the gesture recognition sensitivity is not enough for our target users’ sensitive gestures to be captured. Normally the input for gesture recognition is given through a wearable data glove [4][22]-[24] or a camera [6]-[8][17]. As our target users are immobile, bedridden patients, requesting them to wear a data glove all day to give commands is not user friendly. The necessity for an improved gesture recognizing system compatible with bedridden people arises. An improved gesture recognition algorithm is proposed which is sensitive enough to recognize the gestures done by immobile patients who have a limited range of mobility when compared with normal people. The gestures will be predefined. A doctor will be consulted to specify the possible gestures for our target users. A camera will be used to camera captures the gestures done by the user and the system processes those images through improved gesture recognition and image processing technologies.

Activity Monitoring of the Patient

As the existing solutions do not cover every aspect of a smart home solution for immobile people. Developing and improving abnormal activity detection systems to detect abnormal actives happen by the patient. The pose estimation model will be developed, and a doctor will be consulted to specify some common anomalies of the patients. An overhead camera will be recording the patient activity and those live footage will be sent to the system. The system will be analyzing that footage normal or abnormal by an improved algorithm using the OpenPose estimation method.

Abnormal Behavior Monitoring

When it comes to the activity monitoring and identification of the bedridden patient, this module main objective is to develop an activity monitoring and identification method for the bedridden patient. Activities of the immobile people should be monitored the whole day because they cannot perform activities as usual. Patients are bedridden and it is difficult for them to wear sensor-driven devices, control voicedriven solutions, and give commands using mobile devices. The best solution is to capture their activities through a camera. I developed a method to check whether the patient is sleeping or falling sleepy, and check whether the patient did exercise.

IoT Device/Socket for controlling domestic appliances

The proposed “MobiGo” IoT device solution target the immobile person (disabled people with at least one hand movable), bedridden, etc. With the creation of this IoT device, the day to day activities of the immobile person is much easier. One of the unique features of this “MobiGo” product is the ability to activate electric devices using simple movements while he or she in bed. The camera detects the gestures and resolves it via an algorithm, then sends commands to the smart socket and executes the relevant electric devices. This smart socket is implement using a raspberry pi circuit and it allows us to easily manage electric devices like light, fan, air-condition, and phone charger with a simple gesture.

Methodology


Above figure illustrates the overall system architecture diagram of the proposed system. The target user is the bedridden patient and the gestures, daily activities and abnormal behaviors are capturing through the camera as mentioned in the diagram. The three components are parallelly running on the system. They are gesture recognition algorithms, activity monitoring, and identification algorithm, and abnormal behavior detection and identification algorithm. The video captured by the camera is sent to the central node and performs the main three tasks of the solution mentioned previously. The video capture is sent into the activity monitoring module and abnormal behavior monitoring module to perform tasks like detecting the behaviors, detecting abnormal behaviors, and generating reports, and sending alerts. 27 The video stream sent to the gesture recognition module is first separated into frames and applying the preprocessing functionalities like resizing and grayscale conversion and thresholding to get and black and white output. The frames are then analyzed and predicted using a CNN model trained with some pre-defined gestures whether the hand pose is similar or not. Finally, the central device will perform the relevant command according to the gesture. The switch of the electric device will be switched on or off according to that command. With the central hub, monitoring normal activities and abnormal behaviors parallelly execute and via API s’ the specific message will be informed to the guardian user as SMS.
